Recall by Genotype

Actionable genomic variants in the HRN cohort for proactive participant recall · Ranked by clinical urgency and trial availability

ⓘ How recall variants are selected ›

✓ Inclusion Criteria

  • ✓ Germline variant — identifiable from exome sequencing data at rest in the HRN; not somatic or acquired
  • ✓ Trial eligibility requirement — genotype is an inclusion criterion or strongly preferred for enrollment in an active Phase 2+ or commercial program
  • ✓ Underdiagnosis gap — condition has a substantial diagnosed/undiagnosed gap, meaning HRN recall adds value beyond standard clinical referral
  • ✓ Active clinical action — an approved drug or near-term enrollment window exists; recall translates to a concrete next step for the participant
  • ✓ HRN prevalence threshold — enough expected carriers in the HRN to justify building a recall protocol (generally ≥10 individuals estimated)

✗ Exclusion Criteria

  • ✗ Somatic, tumor-only, or post-zygotic variants not present in germline sequencing
  • ✗ Variants where clinical management would not change based on genotype alone (no treatment or trial available)
  • ✗ PRS-only enrichment without a high-penetrance variant anchor — those belong in the Clinical Development tab as Prognostic Enrichment
  • ✗ Variants too rare in the HRN to yield a meaningful recall cohort (<10 expected individuals)
  • ✗ Somatic variants not present in germline sequencing data, or variants with no plausible therapeutic or research application
ⓘ Helix Readiness levels explained ›

⚡ Engage Now

  • ✓ Variant is well-characterized and reliably called from HRN exome data
  • ✓ Active Phase 2+ trial or approved drug with a direct enrollment path exists
  • ✓ HRN carrier prevalence is sufficient for a meaningful recall cohort
  • ✓ Recall protocol is straightforward to scope — sponsor outreach should begin immediately

🔬 Develop Protocol

  • ● Variant is identifiable in HRN but a formal recall, consent, and recontact protocol needs to be scoped
  • ● Clinical opportunity is confirmed — the work required is operational, not scientific
  • ● Sponsor relationship or trial alignment may need to be established in parallel

📋 Qualify Cohort

  • ● Need to first assess HRN carrier prevalence, ancestry representation, or phenotype depth
  • ● Variant calling pipeline or clinical linkage may need validation before committing to a recall program
  • ● Opportunity is plausible but cohort fit is unconfirmed

⏸ Monitor

  • ● Program is pre-clinical, Phase 1, or has no active enrollment window
  • ● No immediate recall action — revisit when a trial opens or drug advances to Phase 2+
  • ● Useful for target validation, natural history studies, or pipeline awareness
